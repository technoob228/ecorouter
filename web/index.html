Where your 10% goes

Three funds. Monthly rotation. Every cent tracked on-chain.

AI's environmental impact hits three areas: carbon emissions from electricity, freshwater consumed by cooling systems, and ecosystem damage from infrastructure. We partner with one fund for each. The eco wallet accumulates 10% of all deposits throughout the month, then sends a single batch donation on the 1st — publicly visible on the blockchain.

How the rotation works: Each fund receives donations every 3 months. The eco wallet collects fees continuously, and we send a single batch transfer on the 1st of each month to the active fund. One full cycle = 3 months. All transfers are public USDT transactions on the Tron blockchain — anyone can verify them on Tronscan.

FAQ

What models are available?
All major models: GPT-4o, GPT-4o-mini, Claude Sonnet, Claude Opus, Llama, DeepSeek, and more. We route through OpenRouter, so you get access to 200+ models.
How do I add credits?
After signing up, you'll get a USDT (TRC-20) deposit address. Send any amount to that address with your unique cents suffix, and credits appear in your account automatically.
Why 11% more than using providers directly?
10% goes directly to environmental funds on-chain. 1% covers infrastructure and operations. On GPT-4o-mini that's about $0.10 per million tokens — less than a rounding error for most usage.
How do I know the money actually goes to charities?
Every eco wallet transaction is on the Tron blockchain. You can see the wallet address, every incoming fee, and every outgoing donation on Tronscan. We can't fake it even if we wanted to.
Is my data private?
We don't store your conversations. Requests are proxied to AI providers in real time. We only store your email and usage stats for billing.
Which funds receive the donations?
We rotate monthly between three funds, each tied to a specific environmental damage caused by AI: COTAP (carbon offsets via forestry, Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct), WaterAid (clean water access, Feb/May/Aug/Nov), and Oceanic Society (ocean conservation, Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec). Eco wallet accumulates fees all month, then sends one batch donation on the 1st. See the "Where your 10% goes" section above for details.
Who runs this?
One developer. Open source project. No VC funding, no corporation. Just a proxy, two public wallets, and a commitment to transparency.
What happens if the project shuts down?
Remaining eco wallet balance gets donated in full. The wallet is public — you can verify this happened. Code is open source — anyone can fork and continue.
